Our Stakeholders

We are proud to collaborate with these leading companies in the tech and telecommunications sector to drive electronic recycling efforts and promote environmental sustainability.

Vodacom Lesotho

Vodacom Lesotho is working with IWB on a nationwide initiative called "Tech for Earth." This project focuses on collecting and recycling old smartphones from customers, offering incentives like discounted data packages and eco-friendly gadgets in return. Through this collaboration, we aim to reduce e-waste while providing access to cutting-edge technology.

Econet Lesotho

Econet Lesotho partners with IWB for a campaign named "ReTech." The goal of this campaign is to recycle outdated mobile devices and laptops while ensuring responsible disposal of electronic waste. In return, customers who participate are offered a chance to win mobile data and airtime. IWB handles the electronic recycling process, while Econet promotes the initiative to its extensive customer base.

Netback Solutions

Netback Solutions, a leading IT service provider in Lesotho, collaborates with IWB on the "GreenIT" project. This project aims to recycle old tech equipment such as desktops, servers, and networking devices. The initiative ensures that businesses in Lesotho adopt sustainable practices by properly disposing of outdated technology while protecting the environment.

Lesotho Tech Innovations

Lesotho Tech Innovations joins forces with IWB for the "SmartRecycle" project. This project allows schools and universities in Lesotho to donate old computers and laptops for recycling. IWB ensures that all donated devices are properly dismantled, reused, or recycled. In exchange, students and staff receive discounts on tech products and services offered by Lesotho Tech Innovations.
